\## Strategic / Tentpole Features

### Point-in-Time Float — Float as a First-Class Momentum Pillar
Source: surfaced 2026-05-18 during Day 7B float-display bug diagnosis

Possible v0.3.0 tentpole theme: "FugaEdge knows what the float actually was when you traded it."

Concept: For every trade, store and display the actual tradable share count (float) **as it was on the date of the trade** — not today's number, not shares outstanding, not a current snapshot. Treat float as a first-class momentum-trading pillar alongside catalyst, time of day, and price action.

Why this is bigger than a feature:
- Float is one of the core pillars of momentum stock selection (Ross Cameron strategy, the entire DTSM playbook)
- Small-cap momentum names reverse-split, dilute, and re-issue shares constantly — today's float says nothing about what the setup looked like on the day of the trade
- A correct point-in-time float unlocks the Float Bucket Edge Insights idea already in this file, the MFE/reversal tracking idea, and any future dilution-detection or offering-flag features
- No competing journal does this well — most show today's float, or shares outstanding mislabeled as float

What we discovered on 2026-05-18:

FugaEdge v0.1.6 / early-v0.2.0 was displaying shares outstanding (from Polygon's `share_class_shares_outstanding` field via `fetchTickerDetails`) and labeling it "Float." The codebase already had a `fetchFreeFloat` function targeting Polygon's `/stocks/vX/float` endpoint, written and documented but never wired up (zero callsites outside its own definition file).

3-ticker curl test against Polygon's `/stocks/vX/float` on the free tier:

| Ticker | Real float (Finviz) | FugaEdge displayed | Polygon `/stocks/vX/float` returned | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| CISS | 520K | 528K | 2,432,345 | ~4.7× too large; effective_date 2026-03-19 |
| MOBX | 8.72M | 10M | 80,797,136 | ~9.3× too large; effective_date 2026-02-17 |
| LABT | 6.09M | 14M | `results: []` | empty response |

Conclusions:
- The dedicated free-float endpoint exists on the free tier but returns data that is *worse* than what FugaEdge currently shows
- The ratios (~5× CISS, ~10× MOBX) are suspiciously close to clean integers — likely stale, split-unadjusted data
- The `vX` in the URL is a signal: not `v1`/`v2`/`v3`, likely an experimental or low-priority endpoint
- LABT has no float data at all — coverage on small-caps is patchy
- Wiring `fetchFreeFloat` as-is would make the displayed number significantly worse than the shares-outstanding figure that's currently shown

Conclusion: a free-tier Polygon endpoint won't solve this. Point-in-time float needs a real data-sourcing plan.

Data-sourcing options to evaluate (v0.3.0 planning):
1. Polygon paid tier — verify the float endpoint is materially better on paid before committing
2. SEC EDGAR scraping — parse Form 10-Q/10-K/8-K share-count disclosures by filing date; free but multi-day build
3. Alternate vendor (Benzinga, Intrinio, Finnhub historical) — cost vs. coverage trade-off
4. Hybrid — use Polygon's `free_float_percent` × ticker-details shares-outstanding as a derived float; cheap, validated against 2/3 tickers today but needs a larger sample (10+ tickers) before trusting

Good news on the technical side: the DB infrastructure already exists.
- `trades.float_shares` column was added in v0.2.0 Day 1 as a per-trade snapshot (currently populated with shares-outstanding-at-import-time, but the column shape is right)
- The orchestrator pattern (`src/core/float/orchestrator.ts`) is in place
- The user-override path (`FloatEditor.tsx`, `tradeFloatSave` IPC) is in place
- v0.3.0 doesn't need a schema migration; it needs the correct historical value to flow into the existing column

v0.2.0 mitigation (shipped on Day 7C, 2026-05-18):
- Disabled the `runFloatSweetSpot` Edge Insight rule (made analytic claims based on shares-outstanding bucketing)
- Hidden the `FloatBreakdownCard` (subtitle promised "tradable share float at the time of the trade" which was false)
- Relabeled display surfaces from "Float" to "Shares Out" across TradesTable, TradeDetailModal, FloatEditor, DayTradesPanel, Trades.tsx column toggle
- Dropped the word "float" from soft-claim copy in Hot Symbol rule body, Playbook placeholder, onboarding tour, VolumeTab caption
- Release notes name the v0.3.0 point-in-time-float feature as the proper fix

v0.3.0 scope when this ships:
1. Pick a data source from the four options above (decision-tree, not all-of-the-above)
2. Build the enrichment path that fetches historical float for the trade date
3. Re-enable `runFloatSweetSpot` with correct point-in-time data
4. Re-enable `FloatBreakdownCard` with subtitle that's actually true
5. Restore "float" wording across the four soft-claim surfaces that v0.2.0 softened
6. Resolve the two collateral findings v0.2.0 deferred:
   - Bucket-boundary inconsistency between Analytics (4 buckets) and Reports (3 buckets) — unify on a single canonical scheme
   - Per-trade-vs-per-symbol read inconsistency — Analytics reads from `trades.float_shares`, Reports reads from `market_data.float` cache; user overrides only show on one side. Unify to per-trade read everywhere once the trade-level value is correct.
7. Rename DB columns and TypeScript field names from `float` / `float_shares` to something that disambiguates point-in-time-float from current-shares-outstanding (e.g., `float_at_trade_date` and `shares_outstanding_current`)
8. Build the Float Bucket Edge Insights ideas this unlocks (separate parking-lot entry)

Estimated effort: 2-3 weeks depending on data-source choice. SEC EDGAR path is the longest; Polygon paid tier path is the shortest (if it works).

Dependencies satisfied by this feature: Float Bucket Edge Insights, dilution detection, MFE/reversal tracking (all elsewhere in this file).

Possible release home: v0.3.0 tentpole, or shared v0.3.0 theme with shadcn migration / timezone architecture / i18n if those land together.

\### 5 Pillars of Stock Selection — configurable trade-quality framework

Source: Lao, 2026-05-17 (refined during Day 7A scope discussion)



Concept: A configurable framework where users define their 5 (or N) pillars of stock selection — the criteria they use to qualify a momentum setup before entry. Default preset inspired by Ross Cameron / Warrior Trading public criteria. Users can edit, reorder, or replace pillars to match their own edge.



The 5 default Pillars (as Lao defined them):

\- Relative volume (RVOL ≥ 5x the 30-day average)

\- Float (low float preferred for momentum)

\- Price range (typical setup price window)

\- Catalyst (news, earnings, halt resume, etc.)

\- Daily change percentage (gap, % from open, % from premarket)



Why this matters:

\- Ross's Warrior Trading audience already thinks in terms of A+ setups vs lower-quality setups. A pillar framework formalizes that intuition.

\- FugaEdge is positioned for momentum traders — having a built-in selection framework that mirrors how this audience already trades is identity-aligned.

\- Configurability means it works for traders who use other strategies (Tester B's pillars may differ from Tester A's may differ from Lao's).

\- Could become the spine of a richer Edge Insights surface: "Your win rate by pillar-count," "Which pillar matters most to your edge."

\- This idea is what drove the Day 7A Commit 5.5 decision to restore aggregates fetching at import time — without daily_volumes + avg_volume, the RVOL pillar can't score, which would break the feature on day one.



Data dependencies (now ready as of v0.2.0):

\- RVOL: needs `daily_volumes` + `avg_volume` (populated at import as of v0.2.0 Day 7A Commit 5.5)

\- Float: needs `float` (populated at import as of v0.2.0 Day 7A Commit 5 + bug fix)

\- Price range: derivable from trade entry/exit prices (already in trades data)

\- Catalyst: currently manual user tagging; future Polygon News API integration (separate v0.4.1+ ticket)

\- Daily change %: needs intraday open / previous close (requires Polygon aggregates extension)



Direction unresolved — needs decision before scoping:

\- Journal direction: retroactive scoring of imported trades. For each trade, surface which pillars were/weren't present. Edge Insights then correlates pillar-presence with outcome.

\- Screener direction: real-time pre-trade scoring. User enters a candidate ticker during the session, app computes pillar score from live data, helps with go/no-go decision.

\- Hybrid: define pillars once, use them both pre-trade (manual entry) and post-trade (auto-scored on import via Polygon data).



Scope estimate (large, regardless of direction):

\- Pillar definition schema + storage (per-user, editable)

\- Default preset based on Ross's criteria (research required for exact thresholds)

\- Scoring logic per pillar (some auto-computable from Polygon — float, RVOL, daily change; some require user judgment — catalyst quality, chart cleanliness)

\- UI for editing pillars in Settings

\- UI for displaying pillar scores on trade cards

\- Edge Insights rules that use pillar data

\- Onboarding to set defaults



Open questions:

\- v0.3.0 tentpole or v0.4.0+ feature? Likely v0.4.0 — it's a real product surface, not a polish item, and benefits from shadcn migration landing first.

\- Pairs with the existing playbook tag system, or replaces it? Playbooks describe setup type; pillars describe setup quality. Possibly orthogonal, possibly overlapping.

\- Pro tier feature, or free? Probably free for default preset, Pro for custom pillars + advanced insights — TBD.



Risks:

\- Ross attribution: using his framework directly may need permission or careful framing. "Inspired by Ross Cameron's criteria" vs "Ross Cameron's pillars" is a meaningful legal/relationship distinction.

\- Scope: this is a feature, a UI surface, an analytics surface, and an onboarding flow combined. Not a single-day build.



Possible home: v0.4.0+ (deserves its own theme release, not a side-quest)

### Surface import progress in the renderer (IMPORT_PROGRESS consumer)

Source: Day 7A diagnosis, 2026-05-18. The `IMPORT_PROGRESS` channel was declared in commit 3 and is fired from `electron/import/ipc.ts:422` for every per-symbol step in the float and aggregates phases, but the renderer has no listener — confirmed by reading `src/pages/Import.tsx`. The `Saving to database…` spinner transitions away purely when the IPC invoke promise resolves; until then, the user has zero visibility into whether anything is in flight or stuck.

Concept: renderer subscribes to `IMPORT_PROGRESS` and replaces the binary `committing` spinner with a step-aware progress view (parsing → committing → enriching country → enriching float → enriching aggregates → done), surfacing per-symbol counts on the long phases.

Why: better UX on slow imports, and a real diagnostic affordance. Today the user can't tell whether an enrichment is in flight or stuck — last night's 65-NEW spinner hang would have been instantly readable if the spinner had been showing "enriching aggregates 12/65".

Scope: small-to-medium. Channel and emitters already exist; the work is renderer state + UI. Pairs with [[share-ticker-reference-call-between-country-and-float-orchestrators]] because that ticket may reshape the per-phase progress totals.

Possible home: v0.3.0.

\### Auto-isolate dev DB from packaged DB (testing infrastructure)

Source: three distinct failure modes hit in one evening (2026-05-16) around the dev/packaged-build split sharing `%APPDATA%\fugaedge\`. Each one cost real diagnosis time; the auto-isolation work closes all three.



Motivating incidents:

1\. **Test data leaks dev → real dashboard.** Day 2 smoke-test imports (Tester A's CSV scenarios 1-3+6, Tester B's CSVs scenarios 4-5) ran via `npm run dev` against the shared userData path, polluting the real journal dashboard. Required a manual `.bak` restore from `backups/` to recover.

2\. **Packaged shadows dev (binary-vs-DB-version mismatch).** After the restore, the next launch was the installed packaged build (`%LOCALAPPDATA%\Programs\FugaEdge\FugaEdge.exe`, built 2026-05-14 with `SCHEMA_VERSION='17'`) instead of the dev branch (`SCHEMA_VERSION='18'`). Old binary opened the restored v17 DB, saw "current = target", and silently bypassed the v17→v18 migration. No warning that two binaries with different schema targets were writing to the same file. ~30 min misdiagnosed as a schema-code bug before the file timestamp on the .exe gave it away.

3\. **Env-var bleed across dev tooling.** Running schema diagnostics under Electron's ABI required `ELECTRON_RUN_AS_NODE=1` (the better-sqlite3 binary is compiled against Electron 33's ABI via the `rebuild:sqlite` postinstall, not against system Node). The env var leaked from the manual one-liner into a follow-up `npm run dev`, causing Electron to launch in Node mode and `require('electron')` to return the path string instead of the API namespace — opaque TypeError on `protocol.registerSchemesAsPrivileged`. Addressed by `npm run inspect:schema` (commit fef5b20, cross-env-scoped), but the env-var-bleed-via-shared-shell class is the same dev/test isolation theme.



Concept: In dev mode (`!app.isPackaged`), use `%APPDATA%\fugaedge-dev\fugaedge.db` instead of `%APPDATA%\fugaedge\fugaedge.db`. Zero-friction separation between dev/test work and real journal data — `npm run dev` automatically writes to a separate DB without anyone remembering anything. Directly addresses #1 and #2 above.



Belt-and-suspenders: add a `FUGAEDGE_DB_PATH` env var override so multiple fixture sets can each have their own DB when needed (e.g., `FUGAEDGE_DB_PATH=...\tester-b-fixtures.db npm run dev`). Also closes future "I need to test against Tester A's data without touching dev state" scenarios.



Related: any dev script that needs the Electron ABI should be wrapped in a `cross-env`-scoped npm script (precedent: `inspect:schema` at fef5b20) so `ELECTRON_RUN_AS_NODE` never leaks into the user's shell session.



Trade-off to think through before implementing: auto-isolation immediately splits the current real-data dashboard from new dev runs. Anyone who currently uses `npm run dev` against real data (e.g., journaling between dev sessions) loses that view unless they launch the packaged installer. If that's acceptable, ship the auto-isolation. If not, ship env-var-override alone first and add auto-isolation later.



Possible home: v0.3.0 (small infrastructure win, frees future smoke testing from data-pollution risk; three concrete failure modes already paid the diagnosis tax).

\### Per-date DST verification on Webull Mobile timestamps

Source: Day 4 design decision (2026-05-16). The single Webull Mobile fixture covered 2026-04-16 → 2026-05-14 — entirely inside US Daylight Saving Time, so the literal `EDT` suffix in every timestamp was seasonally correct (`UTC-04:00`). Whether Webull emits seasonally-correct abbreviations year-round (i.e. switches to `EST` after the November DST transition) is unverified — we have no winter fixture to confirm.



v0.2.0 parser behavior (already shipped on Track A):

\- Read the literal abbreviation (`EDT` or `EST`) from the timestamp.

\- Convert to UTC accordingly (`-04:00` for EDT, `-05:00` for EST).

\- **No per-date DST verification.** Trust the literal.



v0.3.0 enhancement: for each parsed Webull Mobile timestamp, compute the actual DST status for that date in `America/New_York`. If the literal abbreviation in the file disagrees with the computed status (e.g. file says `EDT` but date is 2026-12-15, which is EST), either:

\- **Reject the row** with a clear error citing the mismatch (strict), OR

\- **Warn and proceed** using the computed offset (lenient, but logs the discrepancy)



Decision between strict/lenient deferred until a real user reports a mismatch — first complaint is the signal, not speculative defense.



Blocks: nothing in v0.2.0. Fallback behavior (trust the literal) is correct in 99%+ of real cases. Only matters if Webull ships stale abbreviations across DST boundaries; no evidence yet that they do.



Estimated effort: <1 day (computed offset is a one-liner with `Intl.DateTimeFormat` or a tiny lookup table for US DST transitions).

\### Store true UTC timestamps + rename column + add display-local rendering

Source: Day 4 design decision (2026-05-16). The column `executions.timestamp_utc` (`schema.ts:69`) is misnamed — all four DAS parsers and the new Webull parsers all store bare local Eastern, not UTC. Cross-format sort works because everyone agrees on the convention, but the column name lies and users in non-Eastern timezones (international users) can't currently see times in their own local time.



Audit evidence:

\- `parse-tradehistory.ts:64-74` (parseTradeHistoryDate) parses `MM/DD/YYYY` as-is, no TZ math.

\- `parse-tradehistory.ts:77+` (parseTradeHistoryTime) returns `HH:MM:SS` unchanged.

\- `parse-trades-window.ts:65-74` (parseBareTime) returns the literal HH:MM:SS unchanged.

\- `Execution.time` type comment says `ISO YYYY-MM-DDTHH:MM:SS` — no `Z`, no offset.

\- The new Webull parsers (Day 4-5) explicitly drop the `EDT`/`EST` literal and the `+0000` offset to match this convention.



Solution:

1\. Add a parser helper `localEasternToUtc(date: string, time: string): string` and apply to all six parsers (4 DAS + 2 Webull). Output: ISO 8601 with `Z` suffix.

2\. Rename column `timestamp_utc` — keep the name now that it's accurate. (Or rename to something else if the column ever needs to hold pre-UTC data; column rename + data migration in same release.)

3\. Add a display-layer helper that renders UTC in the user's preferred timezone (defaulting to America/New_York for the US market-hours convention most active users will want).

4\. Update all parser tests with hardcoded local Eastern output to assert the new UTC values.

5\. Audit downstream consumers (`electron/stats/`, `electron/analytics/`, `electron/calendar/`, `electron/reports/`, `src/core/insights/`) for any time-based logic that assumed local Eastern. Anywhere that compares against market-hour windows (9:30, 11:00, etc.) must convert UTC → Eastern before comparing — likely the largest part of the work.



Estimated effort: 1-2 days. Touches every parser, every parser test, and any downstream consumer that reads `.time` or `timestamp_utc` directly. Bundle with [[per-date-dst-verification]] since both touch timestamp handling — landing them together saves a second audit pass.



Blocks: Nothing in v0.2.0 — the bare-local-Eastern convention is internally consistent. v0.3.0 cleanup, alongside the dev-DB-isolation and paper-account work as part of the "v0.2.0 left these things half-done on purpose" theme.

\### Share ticker-reference call between country and float orchestrators

Source: Day 7A Commit 5.5 scope decision, 2026-05-17. When Commit 5.5 added the aggregates phase to `enrichAfterCommit`, the per-symbol API-call count for the import path went from 2 (pre-Day-7A) to 3 (country, float-via-ticker-details, aggregates). The country and float phases both call Polygon's `/v3/reference/tickers/{symbol}` endpoint — the same endpoint with the same response — once per symbol. One of those two calls is pure waste; both phases extract different slices of the same response payload.



Why it wasn't fixed in Commit 5.5: the clean fix changes the orchestrator topology. Either:



\- merge country + float into one "ticker-details" orchestrator that resolves country, float, market\_cap, sector from a single shared response; OR

\- introduce a per-batch fetch-cache the wrappers consult before hitting Polygon (so country phase populates the cache, float phase reads from it).



Both are wider scope than Day 7A's audit context — orchestrator boundary changes ripple into the composer's typing, the per-phase progress events, error-isolation semantics, and the renderer-side `IMPORT_PROGRESS` consumer (v0.3.0 ticket `[[import-progress-ui-renderer-consumer]]`). Day 7A's ratified scope was "ship the bug fix correctly, defer architectural cleanup."



Impact today: one extra Polygon call per imported symbol. With `REQUEST_SPACING_MS = 350`, that's ~350ms × N extra wall-clock per import. For a 20-symbol first import, ~7 seconds of slack. Free-tier rate limit is the harder concern (5 calls/min), so the 350ms spacing already gates concurrency — the extra call is more of a politeness-budget cost than a user-visible latency cost.



Cross-references — these are the files Commit 5.5 created/touched that will need coordinated changes when this ticket is implemented:



\- `src/core/country/import-orchestrator.ts` — country orchestrator, currently calls `fetchTickerReference` via its `fetchRef` callback.

\- `src/core/float/orchestrator.ts` — float orchestrator, broadened to return `{float, market_cap, sector}` in Commit 5.5; calls `fetchTickerDetails` via its `fetchFloat` callback (which is `fetchTickerReference` + `extractTickerDetails`).

\- `electron/import/resolve-countries.ts` — country wrapper.

\- `electron/import/enrich-float.ts` — float wrapper.

\- `src/core/import/post-commit-enrich.ts` — composer that sequences the phases.



Possible home: v0.3.0 cleanup. Not blocking — current behavior is correct, just wasteful. Pairs naturally with any work that touches the orchestrator topology (e.g. a per-batch caching layer used by `[[import-progress-ui-renderer-consumer]]` for shared progress totals).

### Polygon paid-tier detection + per-tier behavior matrix

Source: Day 7A live evidence, 2026-05-18. Confirmed that Polygon's free tier returns 403 on same-day intraday aggregates. Exact response captured today:

    [FE intraday] CISS 2026-05-18 failed: 403: 403 Forbidden
    {"status":"NOT_AUTHORIZED","request_id":"...","message":"Your plan doesn't include this data time frame. Please upgrade your plan at https://polygon.io/"}

Polygon's Starter tier ($29/mo) unlocks same-day intraday; the behavior matrix and rate-limit budget differ from free.

Concept: on Polygon API-key save, probe a known paid-tier-only endpoint to infer tier, then persist tier in settings. Adjust enrichment behavior accordingly — free tier skips same-day intraday and shows a Settings explainer ("intraday MAE/MFE requires paid Polygon — your other enrichment is working"); paid tier enables all paths and tightens `REQUEST_SPACING_MS` toward the paid quota.

Why: today, free-tier users see `failed=2` in logs with no in-app explanation. Detection lets the UI distinguish "broken" from "your tier doesn't include this data" — important for both trust and the eventual Pro-tier upsell path. Also gates the spacing-tightening change so we don't accidentally rate-limit free-tier users harder.

Scope: medium. Detection probe + settings storage + per-tier branching in the enrichment paths + a Settings UI explainer.

Possible home: v0.3.0 or v0.4.0 — depends on when the Pro tier launches and how it pairs with the SaaS pivot. Pairs with [[saas-architecture-pro-tier]] (once FugaEdge owns the Polygon key, this layer collapses into server-side tier policy).

### SaaS architecture + Pro tier — the v0.4.0 strategic pivot
Source: Lao, 2026-05-18 (decision after Day 7A rate-limit work 
exposed the limitations of bring-your-own-Polygon-key)

**Strategic positioning:** FugaEdge is the niche journal for 
momentum day traders. Not a general journal that supports momentum. 
THE journal for Warrior Trading / small-cap-runner / momentum-
scalping audience. Audience constraint is the moat.

**Architecture pivot:** Move from local-first desktop app with 
bring-your-own-Polygon-key to SaaS with server-side enrichment.

Why this is the right move:
- Every successful trading journal (Tradezella, TraderSync, 
  Edgewonk, TradesViz) does this. The market has spoken.
- "Bring your own API key" is conversion poison. Tester B, a 
  technical user, hit this wall. Less technical users would 
  bounce immediately.
- Pro tier monetization subsidizes Polygon Business tier license.
  Math: 100 users × $29/mo - Polygon Business cost = sustainable.
- Server-side caching means a Polygon call for AAPL serves all 
  users, not one user. Massive cost efficiency at scale.
- Foundation for cloud sync, mobile companion, multi-account.

Components of the v0.4.0 pivot:
1. Backend server (cloud VPS, ~$50-100/month base infrastructure)
2. FugaEdge-server proxy for Polygon (auth, caching, rate-limit 
   management)
3. User authentication / account system
4. Subscription billing (Stripe or similar)
5. Polygon Business tier negotiation (estimated $200-500/month at 
   small scale, negotiable upward at user volume)
6. Tier strategy: free tier with limits, Pro tier (~$29/month) 
   with full enrichment + cloud sync + mobile
7. Data residency / privacy policy (users' trade data now leaves 
   their machine)
8. GDPR / regulatory compliance (FugaEdge would be a data 
   controller)

Audience commitment (the moat):
- Always optimize for momentum day traders first
- Polygon news for catalyst tagging (Pro tier differentiator)
- Pillars of Stock Selection (already filed) becomes the spine
- Edge Insights tuned for momentum-specific patterns
- Default playbooks: Bull Flag 1min, Micro Pullback, Halt Resume, 
  ABCD, parabolic shorts (Warrior taxonomy)
- Saying NO consistently to crypto, options, futures, swing, 
  long-term — these dilute the focus

Risk factors:
- SaaS pivot doubles the engineering surface area
- Customer support becomes a real ongoing job
- Server downtime = user-facing outages (no longer "your local 
  app works fine")
- Liability of holding user trading data
- Competitive pressure: existing SaaS journals have years of 
  head start; differentiation is the niche focus, not feature 
  parity

What this is NOT:
- A v0.3.0 scope item. v0.3.0 stays disciplined: shadcn 
  migration, broker support polish, the four polish tickets 
  filed today.
- A v0.2.0 scope item. We ship v0.2.0 with bring-your-own-key, 
  knowing it's the transitional model.
- A decision to make in one sitting. This is the kind of pivot 
  that's worth a week of dedicated planning before committing.

Pre-v0.4.0 strategic work needed:
- Read Polygon's current Terms of Service in detail
- Email Polygon sales: "I'm building a multi-user trading 
  journal. What licensing tier do I need? Pricing?"
- Competitor analysis: study Tradezella's pricing tiers, 
  Edgewonk's positioning, TraderSync's onboarding flow
- Decide free tier scope (what do you give away to drive 
  acquisition?)
- Decide Pro tier price point (audience can afford $29-49/mo if 
  the product is right)
- Decide pivot timeline: v0.4.0 (next 3-4 months) or sooner?

What pairs naturally with this:
- [[migration-importers]] — switching cost lock-in is the moat 
  for incumbent SaaS journals; one-click migration breaks it
- [[5-pillars-of-stock-selection]] — niche-defining feature
- [[auto-catalyst-detection-from-polygon-news]] — Pro tier 
  differentiator
- [[iphone-companion-app]] — requires cloud sync, requires SaaS
- [[cloud-sync-pro-tier-foundation]] — already filed, this 
  ticket is the strategic frame around it
- [[italian-translation]] — internationalization aligns with 
  niche-but-global audience

Possible home: v0.4.0 launch as "FugaEdge Pro." This is the 
strategic theme of the next major release. Everything in v0.3.0 
should be evaluated through "does this make the eventual v0.4.0 
pivot easier or harder?"

Sleep on it. Decide twice.
