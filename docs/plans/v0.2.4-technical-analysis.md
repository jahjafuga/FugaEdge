# FugaEdge v0.2.4 — "Technical Analysis" Tab Specification

**Bottom line:** Build the Technical Analysis tab as a sibling tab at the top of the Analytics surface, driven by a pre-computed `trade_technicals` table (entry-only indicator state, 1M and 5M), with all six approved sections rendered in a hybrid layout (4 dashboard cards on top, full-width sectioned bands below with inline accordion drill-downs). The single most important design decision is **pre-compute, don't compute-on-query** — the four-bucket grid plus six sections over a 30–90 day window cannot recompute EMAs over thousands of warmup bars on every filter change. Everything else flows from that and from anchoring the feature on Ross Cameron's literal, verbatim rule: *"I only trade when the MACD is positive"* (Warrior Trading, "Diving Into The MACD Scalping Strategy"), which no competitor journal models at the per-trade entry-state level.

## TL;DR

- **Centerpiece feature, top-level placement:** a new Analytics sibling tab whose hero is a four-bucket MACD-state-at-entry grid (Positive+Rising / Positive+Falling / Negative+Rising / Negative+Falling) with win rate, net P&L, avg winner/loser, and expectancy per cell — backed by a stored `trade_technicals` table, not on-the-fly computation.
- **Methodology-true definitions, locked:** "MACD positive" = MACD line above zero (12 EMA > 26 EMA), evaluated at entry only, on a user-toggled 1M (default) / 5M timeframe; "rising/falling" = histogram slope vs the prior bar; VWAP/EMA distances are `(entry − level)/level × 100`. The 4-day warmup cache is more than enough for TradingView-grade MACD accuracy from the first session bar, provided you seed EMAs with the first value (adjust=false) and match session inclusion.
- **Eight build sessions, 1–2 days each:** MACD compute core → technical-state storage → backfill → tab shell + header strip → hero grid + inline-expansion framework → VWAP/EMA bands → combined reads + time-of-day → on-chart MACD toggle (closing beat). Pure TS modules + repository pattern keep it portable to the future Next.js + Postgres / SaaS / iPhone targets.

## A. Exact Metric Definitions

### A1. MACD computation (warmup-wired — bundled into Session 1)
Classic **12 / 26 / 9 on close**, confirmed as Ross's exact settings: *"What are your MACD settings? My MACD settings are standard. They are default. I do not change them."* — Fast Length 12, Slow Length 26, Signal Length 9 (Warrior Trading, "Best MACD Settings for Day Trading").

- EMA multiplier `K = 2 / (period + 1)`.
- Recursive EMA: `EMA_today = (price_today × K) + (EMA_yesterday × (1 − K))`.
- **Seed every EMA (12, 26, and the 9-period signal) with the SMA of the first N values (StockCharts convention).** v0.2.4 ships SMA-seeded for internal consistency: the existing EMA helpers (`src/core/charts/macd.ts` ema() helper, `src/components/trades/ChartTab.tsx` inline ema() for EMA9/EMA20 overlays, `electron/lib/ema.ts`) are all SMA-seeded, so switching only macd.ts would make the MACD sub-pane inconsistent with the EMA9/EMA20 overlays on the same chart. **Warmup washout makes the active-day MACD identical to first-value seeding** (adjust=false): the slow 26-EMA seed weight decays below 0.1% after ~90 bars, and the warmup cache supplies hundreds of bars before the active session opens, so by the first active-day bar the MACD line and signal line have fully converged regardless of seed method. First-value seeding remains the long-term TradingView-parity target, filed as a v0.3.0 cross-helper unification — one shared `/src/core/charts/ema.ts` consumed by macd.ts, the chart inline ema(), and `electron/lib/ema.ts`. See `docs/plans/v0.3.0-or-later-ideas.md` → "TradingView-parity EMA seed unification."
- Signal line = **9-period EMA *of the MACD line*** (not of price).
- Histogram = `MACD_line − signal_line`.

**Warmup sufficiency:** the 4-calendar-day prior-day warmup cache supplies between ~150 and ~1,000+ one-minute bars depending on ticker liquidity in the warmup window (small-cap pre-catalyst tickers are sparse; liquid mid-caps and already-running runners deliver more), with proportional five-minute bars. The slow 26-EMA seed weight decays below 0.1% after ≈90 bars, so for any populated warmup row above that floor the MACD line and signal line have fully converged by the very first bar of the active session. **Warmup length is not the parity risk for typical cases.** The edge case where it IS a risk — warmup populated with fewer than ~90 bars (post-holiday windows collapsed to a single pre-catalyst trading day; e.g. SPRC/Memorial-Day-window with 19 warmup bars) — is documented as a sparse-warmup gap and addressed by the v0.3.0 EMA seed unification (see `docs/plans/v0.3.0-or-later-ideas.md` → "TradingView-parity EMA seed unification"). The two real parity risks remain: (1) seed method (see amended seed bullet above) and (2) session inclusion — matching TradingView's session setting (regular-hours-only vs. including pre/post-market). **v0.2.4 decision: ships with extended hours included** to match the existing chart's candles, VWAP, and EMA9/EMA20 overlays which use the same single Polygon fetch path (`electron/market/massive.ts`) with no session filtering. This keeps the MACD sub-pane internally consistent with the rest of the chart. The residual parity risk is Ross's own TradingView: if his chart has extended hours OFF (the TradingView default), FugaEdge's MACD will diverge slightly on pre/post-market bars. Filed as v0.3.0 evaluation — see `docs/plans/v0.3.0-or-later-ideas.md` → "Regular-session-only MACD — confirm against Ross's TradingView."

### A2. "MACD positive" — LOCKED to Ross's literal rule
Ross's rule is verbatim: *"A key point: I only trade when the MACD is positive. Trading becomes much easier when the MACD is on your side. When it's negative, I avoid taking trades, as the odds are not in my favor."* (Warrior Trading, Medium). "Positive" in his usage = **MACD line above the zero line** (12 EMA above 26 EMA), which is the front-side-of-the-move regime.

Definition: `macd_positive = (macd_line > 0)`.

He separately uses "MACD open" to mean the line is above the signal line (*"I bought the dips as long as MACD remained open, signaling continued momentum"*). These are different concepts, so **store both**:
- `macd_positive = macd_line > 0` (above zero — used for the header strip, discipline score, alignment).
- `macd_open = macd_line > signal_line` (above signal — stored for future use / secondary reads).

The four-bucket grid in Section 2 uses `sign(macd_line)` for the positive/negative axis and histogram slope for the rising/falling axis.

### A3. Rising vs falling
`rising = histogram[entry_bar] > histogram[prior_bar]` (histogram slope). This is the conventional, TradingView-aligned definition — TradingView's default MACD coloring shades bars by `hist[t] > hist[t−1]`, and histogram-slope momentum "fires earlier" than the signal-line crossover. Compare to the prior bar of the **same timeframe** selected by the 1M/5M toggle. If the entry bar is the first bar with valid history (no prior histogram), set `macd_rising = null` and exclude from the rising/falling split (still counts in positive/negative totals).

### A4. VWAP distance
Session VWAP, typical price `hlc3 = (H + L + C) / 3`, cumulative `Σ(hlc3 × volume) / Σ(volume)` from the regular-session open (09:30 ET), reset daily.

`vwap_distance_pct = (entry_price − vwap_at_entry) / vwap_at_entry × 100`

`above_vwap = vwap_distance_pct > 0`.

**Distance-bucket design notes (v1, shared by §A4 and §A5).** The bucket specs below — VWAP distance here, 9 EMA distance in §A5 — partition the signed `*_distance_pct` value defined above (negative = below the level, positive = above), and are **asymmetric by design**: edges weight the above-equilibrium side, reflecting Ross Cameron's "VWAP is the equilibrium" framing — the long edge clusters just above the level while the risk lives in the extended-above tail, so the above-side gets finer resolution. The boundaries are **informed v1 defaults, not industry standards** — from a research-locked report (2026-06-10) on small-cap momentum (Ross Cameron / DTSM), competitive journal tooling, and the thin published distance-vs-win-rate evidence; no named small-cap trader publishes hard percentage thresholds. **Recalibration trigger:** at ~200–500 trades per user, move the edges to that user's empirical quantiles. v1 buckets raw signed percentage only; **ATR-normalized distance** (to neutralize cross-ticker volatility) is a v2 candidate if raw-% buckets show high within-bucket win-rate variance correlated with stock volatility.

**Bucket boundaries (v1):**

| # | Bucket label | Range (%) | Rationale |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Below / broken | < −1.0% | Long thesis broken in Cameron's framework (price lost equilibrium); group all deep-below entries |
| 2 | Just below VWAP | −1.0% to −0.25% | The reclaim/bounce staging zone; tight sub-VWAP consolidation is "1-2% of price" |
| 3 | At VWAP (equilibrium) | −0.25% to +0.25% | "Right at VWAP" — the highest-confluence pullback/reclaim entry band |
| 4 | Slightly extended | +0.25% to +1.0% | Normal healthy entry just above equilibrium |
| 5 | Extended | +1.0% to +3.0% | Getting stretched; watch for first sign of reversion |
| 6 | Stretched / chasing | +3.0% to +6.0% | "Chase" zone (blogs cite 3-4%+); expect deteriorating R:R |
| 7 | Parabolic / danger | > +6.0% | Climactic; long entries here are high-risk, prime reversion territory |

Edges are **left-inclusive, right-exclusive**: a row `a% to b%` means `a% ≤ x < b%` (e.g. bucket 3, At VWAP, is `−0.25% ≤ x < +0.25%`). Every value lands in exactly one bucket.

### A5. EMA distance
For 9 EMA and 20 EMA on the toggled timeframe:

`ema_distance_pct = (entry_price − ema_value) / ema_value × 100`

`above_9ema = ema9_distance_pct > 0`; crossover state `ema9_above_ema20 = (ema9 − ema20) > 0` (bullish stack when true).

**Bucket boundaries (v1):**

| # | Bucket label | Range (%) | Rationale |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Below 9 EMA / broken trend | < −0.5% | "Riding the 9" broken; momentum fading / invalidation |
| 2 | At 9 EMA (pullback zone) | −0.5% to +0.5% | The literal "wait for the pullback to 9 EMA" target — highest-probability band |
| 3 | Slightly above | +0.5% to +2.0% | Normal continuation entry off a shallow pullback |
| 4 | Extended | +2.0% to +5.0% | Starting to chase; "floating above the 9 MA" begins here |
| 5 | Very extended | +5.0% to +10.0% | Parabolic risk; poor pullback entries |
| 6 | Blow-off / parabolic | > +10.0% | Climactic extension; reversion-prone |

Same left-inclusive, right-exclusive convention as §A4.

**20 EMA — binary crossover only (v1):** 20 EMA distance is **not** bucketed in v1. The decision-relevant 20 EMA signal is the 9/20 stacking order, tracked as the binary `ema9_above_ema20 = (ema9 − ema20) > 0` (defined above — stacked-bullish when true, broken when false). Distance-from-20-EMA is largely collinear with distance-from-9-EMA for momentum entries, so bucketing it would split the sample for little marginal insight. Trigger to add 20 EMA buckets later: if the 9/20 binary shows a large win-rate gap **and** users request finer resolution.

### A6. Multi-fill entries — the evaluation point
Use **`entry_price` = volume-weighted average of all entry-side fills** for the distance numerators, but read the **indicator values (MACD/VWAP/EMA) from the bar that contains the FIRST entry fill timestamp.** Rationale: Ross's rule is an entry-decision filter, and the decision happens at the first fill; the average price reflects the position actually taken. This is a defensible, consistent convention. (Alternative considered: weight indicator state by fill — rejected as overcomplex and not how a trader reads the chart at the entry moment.)

### A7. Win rate
`win_rate = count(net P&L > 0) / total trades`. Breakeven ($0 net) counts as a **loss**. Confirmed.

### A8. Expectancy
`Expectancy = (win_rate × avg_winner) − (loss_rate × avg_loss)`

where `loss_rate = 1 − win_rate`, `avg_loss` expressed as a **positive** number, per-trade dollars. Confirmed standard (Van Tharp / TradeZella form). Example: 47 winners avg +$480, 73 losers avg −$190, 120 trades → win_rate 0.392, loss_rate 0.608 → `(0.392 × 480) − (0.608 × 190) = 188.2 − 115.5 = +$72.64` per trade. Suppress display below n=5 (show "—"); show `n=X` always so the user weighs small samples.

### A9. Discipline score
`discipline_score = % of trades where macd_positive AND above_vwap AND above_9ema`, all at entry on the toggled timeframe. The card shows the percentage plus that aligned subset's win rate and net P&L. This is the single number that operationalizes Ross's "full alignment" guidance.

## B. UI Layout (Hybrid)

**Viewport targets (Electron windowed, portable to web):** design for **min 1024px, typical 1280–1440px, max 1920px**. Below 1024px, cards stack 2-wide; below 768px, 1-wide. Use a max content width of ~1680px with centered gutters so it doesn't sprawl on ultrawide.

**Vertical order, top to bottom:**

1. **Filter bar (sticky on scroll, ~56px):** date-range presets [Today / 7D / **30D default** / 90D / YTD / Custom], ticker filter, playbook filter, and the **1M/5M segmented toggle** right-aligned. Mirrors the existing Deep Analytics filter conventions. An excluded-data chip (Section C) and an "Include paper trades" toggle (off) also live here. *(the "Include paper trades" toggle is deferred to v0.3.0+ — see §C.)*

2. **Header Strip (Section 1) — dashboard-card density:** a single row of **4 cards** (~120px tall) spanning full width. Cards: "MACD positive at entry", "Above VWAP at entry", "Above 9 EMA at entry", "Discipline score (full alignment)". Each: large % number, sub-line with subset win rate + net P&L. At <1280px the row wraps to 2×2.

3. **Sectioned bands (Sections 2–6) — full-width:** stacked bands, each with a section header (title + one-line plain-language question it answers) and its grid/bucket content. The hero **MACD State grid (Section 2)** sits immediately under the header strip.

**Inline expansion behavior (LOCKED interaction):** clicking any bucket/cell expands an accordion panel **directly beneath that row**, animating height ~200ms ease-out and pushing lower content (and lower sections) down. No modal, no full-tab swap; all other sections stay mounted and visible. The expanded panel contains a **trade table: 8 rows by default**, a "Show all N" expander, **sortable** columns (date, net P&L, the relevant distance/MACD value), and each row click opens a read-only trade detail sheet (a per-trade drill-down; the full editable TradeDetailModal remains the Trades-tab workflow). Rule: only one expansion open per section at a time (opening a second bucket in the same section closes the first); different sections may be open simultaneously.

## C. Edge Cases

- **Missing indicator data** (legacy pre-feature trades, missing intraday bars, fetch failures): exclude from all technical buckets, but **never silently.** Show a persistent chip in the filter bar — "N trades excluded (no indicator data)" — with a tooltip explaining why. Header-strip percentages are computed over trades *with* data, labeled "(of N trades with data)" so the denominator is honest.
- **Sparse buckets:** always render every bucket (a stable, consistent grid is more legible than a collapsing one). Flag any bucket with **<5 trades** with a muted "low sample" badge, and **suppress expectancy** (show "—") below n=5 since it is statistical noise (reliable expectancy wants 30+ trades). Always show `n=X`.
- **Single-ticker / tiny filters:** degrade gracefully — buckets render with low-sample flags, header cards show small denominators. No hard minimum trade count; the user is trusted to read the n-counts.
- **Paper trades:** **excluded by default**, consistent with v0.2.4's separate paper-trade handling. Provide an "Include paper trades" toggle in the filter bar (off by default). Keep live and paper edges separate so the discipline read isn't polluted by sim behavior. *(Deferred to v0.3.0+ — did not ship in Session 4's filter bar (`c88e4a7`); rides on the broader "Paper-account trade import + filtering" item in `docs/plans/v0.3.0-or-later-ideas.md`. Paper imports are gated off until then, so v0.2.4 has no paper trades for this toggle to filter. The design intent above stands.)*

## E. Data Computation Strategy

**Decision: pre-compute and store per-trade indicator state in a `trade_technicals` table** keyed by `trade_id`. Do **not** compute on-the-fly at query time. Reasoning: the four-bucket grid plus Sections 3–6, recomputed on every tab open and every filter change, would require recomputing EMAs over thousands of warmup bars per trade — unacceptable latency. Pre-compute also makes the values stable, auditable, and directly syncable to future SaaS/mobile targets.

**SaaS-port shape (recorded for future-us):** the lazy-guard + backfill compute path chosen above is the SaaS-shape-aligned choice. In the eventual Next.js + Postgres web app, there is no Electron "import pipeline" — CSV uploads become server-side async jobs, and the equivalent of FugaEdge's lazy guard is a "compute on first read" cache-warm + a scheduled backfill worker. The lazy-guard primitive built in v0.2.4 maps directly to the server-side compute primitive in the web port, with the pure `computeTradeTechnicals` module running unchanged in either deployment target (zero Electron / DB imports per ARCHITECTURE rule #1). Building Session 2 around the lazy guard rather than an Electron-specific import-pipeline hook is therefore the path-of-least-rewrite for the future port.

**Pure module:** `computeTradeTechnicals(trade, bars) → TradeTechnicals` (no DB/Electron imports).

**Stored columns** (entry only, per LOCKED decision — halves storage/compute), for **each timeframe (1M, 5M)**:
`macd_line, signal_line, histogram, histogram_prior, macd_positive, macd_open, macd_rising, vwap, vwap_dist_pct, ema9, ema9_dist_pct, ema20, ema20_dist_pct, ema9_above_ema20`
plus per-row: `data_complete` (bool), `computed_at`, `schema_version`.

**When it runs:** the primary compute path is **lazy-guard + chunked background backfill**, not synchronous on import. This matches the existing codebase reality — `refreshIntraday()` is fire-and-forget after import-commit and explicitly writes `warmup_bars: []`; prior-day warmup bars are populated lazily on first chart open via `getIntradayBars()` silent-backfill. A "compute inline after warmup enrichment" pipeline shape does not exist on disk, and building one would couple Session 2 to Electron-specific import plumbing that does not survive the SaaS port.

The two real triggers:
1. **Lazy guard on chart / Technical Analysis tab open:** any trade whose `trade_technicals` row is missing, has `data_complete = false`, or has a stale `schema_version` is enqueued for background compute. After warmup bars are populated by the on-demand fetch path, the compute runs and the row is upserted. The Technical Analysis tab reads `trade_technicals` only — if a row is missing it shows the trade as "compute pending" rather than blocking the UI.
2. **One-time chunked background backfill on v0.2.4 first launch:** sweeps every historical trade, fetching warmup bars on demand if not cached, computing `trade_technicals` rows in batches (~50 trades/tick, progress toast, cancelable / resumable). After backfill completes, the lazy guard handles new imports and stale rows.

Newly imported trades therefore go through the lazy-guard path on their first chart open, exactly like every other trade. No special-casing of the import-pipeline integration is needed.

**Backfill strategy:** on first launch of v0.2.4 (after the Technical Analysis tab ships), run a **one-time chunked background backfill** over all historical trades (~50 trades/tick, progress toast, cancelable/resumable). Trades whose warmup bars cannot be fetched are marked `data_complete = false` and feed the excluded-data chip. After backfill, newly imported trades compute inline. Recompute-all is also triggered automatically when `schema_version` increments (e.g., a definition change).

## G. Visual Design Guidance

**MACD State grid palette** — match the already-shipped chart histogram colors, which are the lightweight-charts library defaults (confirmed in `tradingview/lightweight-charts` source `series-options.ts`: upColor default `#26a69a`, downColor default `#ef5350`):

| Bucket | Semantics | Color |
|---|---|---|
| **Positive + Rising** | Best — MACD in favor, momentum building (Ross's ideal) | strong green `#26a69a` |
| **Positive + Falling** | Caution — still positive but momentum fading | amber `#f5b041` |
| **Negative + Rising** | Recovering but still below zero — Ross says don't trade yet | muted gray-amber `#aab7b8` |
| **Negative + Falling** | Worst — Ross's explicit avoid zone | strong red `#ef5350` |

Apply the semantic color as a low-opacity (~0.12) cell-background tint; keep metric text high-contrast neutral so numbers stay readable on every tint.

**Iconography:** ▲ / ▼ for rising/falling; a small zero-line glyph for positive/negative; VWAP/EMA distance rows use a centered diverging bar (left = below the level, right = above). Flat, single-weight icons only — match the existing analytics aesthetic.

**Number formatting:**
- P&L: sign always shown, thousands separator — `+$1,250`, `−$890`; cents only below $1,000 (`−$42.50`), no cents at/above $1,000.
- Percentages: 1 decimal — `62.5%`. Distances signed — `+2.3%`, `−1.1%`.
- Expectancy: 2 decimals (per-trade) — `+$72.64`.
- Win rate: 1 decimal.
- Large numbers: abbreviate ≥$10,000 as `$12.5k` in compact cards, full value in tables and tooltips. Counts always integer.
- Divide-by-zero guard: 0 trades → "—" everywhere.

**Empty states:** zero trades after filter → centered illustration + "No trades match these filters" + "Reset filters" button. Per-bucket empty → grayed cell showing `0`, never blank. Low-sample buckets → "low sample" badge + `n=X`.

## H. On-Chart MACD Toggle (closing beat — Session 8)

- **Location:** a **4th row inside the existing "Indicators 3/3" dropdown** (label flips to "Indicators 4/4" when on). Not a separate button — keeps the chart toolbar clean and consistent with the existing indicator-management UX.
- **Persistence:** **global per-user preference** saved to settings (same store as the other indicator toggles), applied to every trade-detail modal. Not per-trade, not per-modal. Rationale: a trader either reads MACD or doesn't; per-trade state would be annoying churn.
- **Default on first launch:** **MACD pane SHOWN (on).** It is the v0.2.4 hero indicator and the basis of the whole tab; defaulting it on showcases the feature and matches Ross's actual workflow (he runs MACD at the bottom of his 1-minute chart). Fully hideable.
- **Interaction with the existing 1M/5M timeframe toggle:** the chart's timeframe toggle drives which timeframe's MACD renders; the pane recomputes from the warmup cache on switch. The **Technical Analysis tab's** 1M/5M toggle is an independent context (analytics bucketing vs. chart rendering) — they do not share state, which avoids confusing coupling between "what I'm charting" and "what I'm analyzing."

## I. Ross Cameron / DTSM Methodology — Verification & Competitive Wedge

**All definitions verified against Warrior Trading primary sources:**
- MACD rule (verbatim): *"I only trade when the MACD is positive… When it's negative, I avoid taking trades, as the odds are not in my favor."*

## Warmup-bars gap (post-5b.1 diagnostic finding)

Diagnostic capture (dev DB `%APPDATA%/fugaedge/fugaedge.db`, 98 round trips, 2026-06-09), recorded so the investigation survives between sessions. The §E backfill pipeline runs, but the Technical Analysis tab shows **68 of 98 trades excluded** ("no indicator data") because their `trade_technicals` rows are stubs (`data_complete = 0`, all six indicator fields null). §E's "Backfill strategy" already notes that warmup-unfetchable trades stay `data_complete = false` — but it presumed warmup *would* be fetched in bulk, and no such fetch was ever built. That is the gap.

- **The 68-stub partition** (read-only SQL over `trades ⋈ trade_technicals ⋈ intraday_bars`):
  - **3** — no `intraday_bars` row at all (`refreshIntraday` never fetched these).
  - **24** — row exists but errored or empty bars (failed fetch; likely halted/delisted symbols, or dates outside Massive's coverage).
  - **41 (≈60% — the lever)** — active-session bars present, but `warmup_bars` empty.

- **The 41-case root cause — warmup is fetched on chart-open only.** Warmup bars are populated *exclusively* by `getIntradayBars` (`electron/market/bars-get.ts:28+` — `fetchWarmupBars` + the silent backfill of legacy rows), which runs when a trade's **chart is opened**. `refreshIntraday` explicitly does **not** fetch warmup: `electron/market/intraday.ts:148` upserts `warmup_bars: []` and fetches only the active session (in-source: *"Proactive warmup-on-refresh is deferred."*). **No bulk warmup backfill exists anywhere.** The launch auto-armed `runTradeTechnicalsBackfill` only *reads* cached warmup (`electron/technicals/backfill.ts:69`) — it never fetches it. With warmup empty, `computeTradeTechnicals` can't seed the MACD EMAs and returns a `data_complete = false` placeholder.

- **Why the 30 complete trades ARE complete:** they are exactly the trades whose **charts have been opened**. Chart-open → `getIntradayBars` fetches the missing warmup and upserts it via `upsertIntradayRow` → the lazy-guard (`electron/technicals/lazy-guard.ts`, gated on warmup-present) recomputes the row to `data_complete = true`. The split is exact and confirmed by query: **30/30 complete trades have warmup bars; 41/41 active-bars-but-stub trades have empty warmup.**

- **Fix shape — build `runWarmupBackfill`.** A bulk pass that enumerates `(symbol, date)` keys with empty `warmup_bars`, calls the existing `fetchWarmupBars` (`bars-get.ts:28` — the per-chart implementation is the building block, already in place), and upserts the warmup via `upsertIntradayRow`. Chain it off `refreshIntraday` completion **and** auto-arm it on launch ahead of the existing `runTradeTechnicalsBackfill` (which then recomputes the 41 to complete). Mirror the technicals-backfill shape: chunked + progress-emitting + dataVersion-bumped. (The 24 errored fetches + 3 no-row still need active-bar retries and may be genuinely unavailable — halted/delisted/out-of-coverage.)

- **Footgun to address — forced refresh wipes warmup.** `refreshIntraday({ force: true })` (the Settings "Force re-fetch" path) sets `warmup_bars: []` (`intraday.ts:148`). The in-source comment claims warmup "re-derives on next access" — true only *per-chart*, not in bulk. A user who sees "73 excluded" and reaches for a forced refresh (the obvious instinct) would **wipe warmup for the 30 currently-complete trades**, demoting them back to stubs until each chart is individually re-opened. Three options: (a) chain `force: true` into `runWarmupBackfill` so the wipe is immediately followed by a refetch; (b) warn before a forced refresh in the UI; (c) stop zeroing warmup on `force: true`.

- **UX gap — no "computing N trades" visibility.** Even with a working backfill, nothing tells the user indicators are being computed for N trades; the excluded count just shrinks silently over time. A Settings status indicator, or an in-tab banner while a backfill is active, would close the loop.

- **Decision locked (2026-06-09):** build `runWarmupBackfill` as **v0.2.4 ship-blocker work** (delaying 5b.2 / Section 3) — beta-tester credibility (Brendan, Dave, Edwin hit this on first import) outweighed ship velocity. Spec in **§K** below.

## K. Warmup-bars backfill (v0.2.4 ship-blocker)

**Why:** the *Warmup-bars gap* finding above stranded 41 of 98 trades as `data_complete = 0` stubs purely because warmup bars are fetched on chart-open only, with no bulk pass. `runWarmupBackfill` is that bulk pass — now canonical v0.2.4 scope, parallel to §A–J.

- **Location — `electron/market/`** (alongside `bars-get.ts` / `intraday.ts`), not `electron/technicals/`. It operates **entirely on `intraday_bars`** (read empty-warmup keys → `fetchWarmupBars` → `upsertIntradayRow`) with zero `trade_technicals` involvement, so it's the bulk sibling of `refreshIntraday`'s active-bar fetch and imports nothing cross-layer; the technicals sweep simply benefits from the populated warmup afterward.

- **Does NOT bump `dataVersion`.** Mirrors `runBackfillCore` semantics — only the technicals-backfill wrapper bumps `dataVersion` after recomputing `trade_technicals`. Warmup writes to `intraday_bars` only, which has no renderer-visible reactivity bound to it; the subsequent `runTradeTechnicalsBackfill` (in the same launch) handles renderer notification when technicals flip.

- **Schema migration — new column `warmup_attempted_at TEXT`.** `ALTER TABLE intraday_bars ADD COLUMN warmup_attempted_at TEXT` (default NULL via column nullability). **`TEXT`, not `TIMESTAMP`** — every ISO-timestamp column in this schema is `TEXT`, and SQLite assigns `TIMESTAMP` NUMERIC affinity, wrong for the ISO string (`new Date().toISOString()`) we store. Set on every attempted warmup fetch — success OR empty-result OR error — so the predicate distinguishes "never tried" from "tried, came back empty." Without this, holiday-window or out-of-coverage keys would re-fetch every launch forever (real API-call cost, especially at scale: Brendan/Dave 500+ trades × free-tier rate limit). Bump `SCHEMA_VERSION`; idempotent guarded ALTER (PRAGMA-checked) per the existing migration pattern. *(§K.1 supersession: §K.1.0 added a second marker column `warmup_error` via a v27→v28 migration, and the §K.1 arc uses it to distinguish a thrown fetch from a legit empty — so the "success OR empty-result OR error" lumping here is the pre-§K.1 form. See §K.1.)*

- **Shape — mirrors `runTradeTechnicalsBackfill`.** Thin orchestration wrapper around a chunked core: 50 keys/chunk per `runChunkedBackfill`'s constant, per-item `try/catch` (failures counted, never fatal), `setImmediate`-yielded between chunks, emits a new `WARMUP_BACKFILL_PROGRESS` channel per chunk. Fast no-op return when the eligible set is empty (the steady state after the first run).

- **Input — eligibility predicate.** Enumerate `(symbol, date)` from `intraday_bars` where `warmup_attempted_at IS NULL AND (warmup_bars IS NULL OR warmup_bars = '[]') AND bars != '[]' AND error IS NULL`. The warmup_attempted_at clause prevents the futile retry loop; the rest matches the §K diagnostic finding. Multiple trades share a `(symbol, date)` key, so the fetch runs **once per symbol-day**, not once per trade. *(§K.1.1 widened the first clause from `warmup_attempted_at IS NULL` to `warmup_attempted_at IS NULL OR warmup_error IS NOT NULL` so errored keys re-enter the worklist; the predicate above is the pre-§K.1 form. See §K.1.)*

- **Per chunk.** For each key:
  1. `getIntradayRow(symbol, date)` to fetch the existing row (must preserve `bars` + `fetched_at` since `upsertIntradayRow` overwrites all columns — see precedent at `bars-get.ts:100-103`).
  2. `fetchWarmupBars(apiKey, symbol, date)` — exported from `bars-get.ts` (currently module-private; export needed). Reads `polygon_api_key` from `getSettings()`. Throws on network failure (recorded as error count, key skipped).
  3. `upsertIntradayRow({ symbol, date, bars: cached.bars, warmup_bars: result, fetched_at: cached.fetched_at, error: null, warmup_attempted_at: new Date().toISOString() })` — re-passes cached active bars + fetched_at unchanged, writes new warmup_bars + warmup_attempted_at marker. *(§K.1.2 added `warmup_error` to this `upsertIntradayRow` call — the thrown message on throw, `null` on success/empty; the field list here is the pre-§K.1.2 form. See §K.1.)*
  4. If the fetch threw or returned empty, `warmup_attempted_at` is still set (the marker means "attempted," not "succeeded"). Empty result is a legitimate outcome for holiday-window or out-of-coverage dates. *(Superseded in §K.1: a thrown fetch now stamps `warmup_error` (§K.1.2) and is retried on the next pass (§K.1.1) rather than being locked like a legit empty. See §K.1.)*

- **Trigger wiring — launch arming.** Replace **only the technicals `setImmediate`** at `electron/main/index.ts:166` with a single awaited async block — leave the sibling `setImmediate(runPendingMaeMfeBackfill)` untouched (it's the independent v0.2.3 mae/mfe recompute, unrelated to the warmup→technicals chain; folding it in would silently drop it):

```ts
  setImmediate(async () => {
    try {
      await runWarmupBackfill({ onProgress: emitWarmupProgress })
      await runTradeTechnicalsBackfill({ onProgress: emitTechnicalsProgress })
    } catch (err) { /* log only */ }
  })
```

  The `await` is load-bearing — separate `setImmediate`s wouldn't guarantee that the warmup pass completes before technicals starts, so the technicals sweep could find still-empty warmup and write more placeholders. The single-async-block awaited pattern is the only honest sequencing.

- **Trigger wiring — `refreshIntraday` completion chain.** Chain `runWarmupBackfill` **then** `runTradeTechnicalsBackfill` in the `MARKET_INTRADAY_REFRESH` IPC handler (`electron/market/ipc.ts`), **not** inside `intraday.ts` — that module is web-portable by design (no `electron` / `wc.send`; progress flows through an injected `emitProgress` callback), and chaining there would leak electron concerns into the portable layer. The handler `await`s `refreshIntraday`'s full resolution, so one chain naturally covers BOTH internal completion paths (the main worker-pool path AND the `attempted === 0` early-return — a refresh that fetches nothing still populates warmup for pre-existing empty-warmup rows). Order is load-bearing (mirrors the launch arming): warmup populates `warmup_bars`, then technicals consumes it to flip stub trades `data_complete 0 → 1` — without the technicals leg, "resolves in-session" is hollow (the rows stay excluded until next launch). Gated on `!result.cancelled` for parity with the inner `ema9Distances` / `maeMfe` chains, and `try/catch`-wrapped (log only) so a catastrophic backfill throw never rejects the handler's promise — `refreshIntraday`'s successful result is preserved and returned to the renderer.

- **`force: true` footgun — resolution (a) via completion chain.** Because the refresh IPC handler now runs the warmup → technicals chain on every non-cancelled completion, a forced refresh (`force: true`) that zeroes `warmup_bars` (`intraday.ts:148`) is immediately followed by the warmup repopulation pass. No special-case branch needed — the chain naturally handles it. Note: forced refresh also wipes `warmup_attempted_at` (re-marked NULL on the `upsertIntradayRow` from `fetchOne`), so the eligibility predicate finds those keys again. Verified safe.

- **Progress channel — net-new IPC plumbing.** New `WARMUP_BACKFILL_PROGRESS = 'market:warmupBackfillProgress'` in `shared/ipc-channels.ts`. Payload `WarmupBackfillProgress { tradesDone: number; tradesTotal: number }` — **trade counts, not key counts** (matches the user's mental model in the excluded-chip count). The orchestrator does the keys→trades join — it's the only layer holding the worklist keys (the IPC emitters and renderer forward the payload generically). `tradeCountsByKey` in `electron/market/repo.ts` queries non-deleted trades grouped by `(symbol, date)` and JS-filters to the worklist; the orchestrator sums per-key counts into `tradesTotal` and accumulates them per chunk for `tradesDone`. New `ipc.warmupOnBackfillProgress(handler)` renderer helper (modeled on `ipc.countryOnBackfillProgress`).

- **Settings status surface.** Add an "Indicators" row to `DataBackfillCard.tsx` that subscribes to `warmupOnBackfillProgress` and renders **"Computing N trades..."** when `tradesTotal > 0 && tradesDone < tradesTotal`, otherwise hidden. The decrement (tradesDone → tradesTotal) is the visible signal. No in-tab Technicals banner — the excluded-data chip already surfaces the count.

- **Acceptance criteria.**
  - Fresh DB, 100 trades imported → after launch completes, **every eligible trade** (active bars present, not halted/delisted/out-of-coverage) reaches `data_complete = true` in the same launch.
  - Errored-fetch / no-row trades (the 24 + 3 in the current dev DB) stay excluded **honestly** — those are upstream intraday-fetch issues, out of scope for warmup backfill.
  - Holiday-window / out-of-coverage keys with empty warmup results are marked `warmup_attempted_at IS NOT NULL` after first attempt and skipped on subsequent launches — no futile retry loop.
  - A Settings **force-refresh does not demote** currently-complete trades to stubs (the chained warmup refetch repopulates them).
  - The Technicals tab's excluded count reflects **only genuinely unrecoverable** trades — never "not yet computed."
  - Progress channel emits trade counts (not key counts) to match user-visible exclusion mental model.

### K.1 — error-retry predicate (`warmup_error`)

§K's original marker logic stamped `warmup_attempted_at` on every attempt — success, empty, *or* throw — and the eligibility predicate (above) treated any stamped key as done, so a key that threw looked identical to a legitimately-empty one and was never retried. Beat 2.7's Smoke 2 (marker re-clear + re-launch, 2026-06-10) exposed how costly that was: roughly **75% of the supposed "empties" were actually transient Polygon free-tier rate-limit throws** stamped as legit empties. §K.1 fixes this by adding a `warmup_error TEXT` column (schema v27→v28), having `runWarmupBackfill` stamp the thrown message there (null on success/empty), and widening the predicate from `warmup_attempted_at IS NULL` to **`warmup_attempted_at IS NULL OR warmup_error IS NOT NULL`** — so errored keys re-enter the worklist while legit empties stay locked. Implemented across `4075e6c` (§K.1.0 — schema migration v27→v28) → `640826d` (§K.1.1 — repo retry predicate) → `1c35089` (§K.1.2 — orchestrator catch-block stamp).

#### K.1.3 — Stranded pre-§K.1.0 throw recovery

**The orphan problem.** Keys that *threw* during the pre-launch smoke runs **before** the `warmup_error` column existed (Beat 2.7's Smoke 2 marker-clear + re-launch, 2026-06-10) now carry `warmup_attempted_at` SET, `warmup_bars = '[]'` (the throw left them empty), and `warmup_error IS NULL` (no column yet to record the throw). To the §K.1 predicate (`warmup_attempted_at IS NULL OR warmup_error IS NOT NULL`) both clauses are false — attempted, no recorded error — so they read as legit-empties and stay locked. The §K.1 arc (`4075e6c` → `1c35089`) distinguishes throws from empties *going forward* but cannot retroactively label a throw it never recorded, so it does **not** recover them. The affected surface is small and install-specific: the **~11 rows on Lao's local dev DB** used to develop the §K.1 arc; zero for fresh installers; a similar handful for testers who ran §K-arc dev builds before 2026-06-10.

**Mitigation — one-time SQL marker re-clear.** Null the marker on exactly the stranded shape, so the §K.1 predicate re-includes those keys on the next `runWarmupBackfill` pass:

```sql
UPDATE intraday_bars
SET warmup_attempted_at = NULL
WHERE warmup_attempted_at IS NOT NULL
  AND warmup_bars = '[]'
  AND warmup_error IS NULL;
```

**Why NOT auto-fold into the §K.1.0 migration.** A fresh user upgrading v27→v28 has zero §K-arc smoke history, so *every* row of that shape (attempted + empty + no error) is a **legitimate empty** from normal use. Running this UPDATE inside the migration would force them to re-fetch every legit empty — burning Polygon quota and triggering the exact rate-limit throws §K.1 was built to prevent. The recovery must therefore be **user-gated, not automatic.**

**Deferred implementation — §K.1.4.** Two options, decision deferred to the actual affected-user surface:
- **Option A — release-note instruction.** Tell affected v0.2.4 users to run the SQL manually. Lightweight, but assumes SQL comfort.
- **Option B — Settings "Recover stranded warmup keys" command.** A guarded button that runs the SQL and shows the affected-row count. Heavier, but user-safe.

Decision pending in §K.1.4; parked in `docs/plans/v0.3.0-or-later-ideas.md`.

### Implementation invariants

Each assertion is concrete enough to name a unit or visual-regression test.

**A. Bucket membership (mechanical).**

1. Left-inclusive, right-exclusive: `vwap_distance_pct = +0.25%` → bucket 4 (Slightly extended), **not** bucket 3.
2. `vwap_distance_pct = −0.25%` → bucket 3 (At VWAP), **not** bucket 2 — `−0.25%` is bucket 3's inclusive lower edge.
3. `ema_distance_pct = +0.5%` → 9-EMA bucket 3 (Slightly above), **not** bucket 2 (same edge rule).
4. **Bucket membership ≠ the `above_*` binary.** `above_vwap` is `vwap_distance_pct > 0` (strict): true for VWAP buckets 4–7, false for 1–2, **mixed within bucket 3** (true iff `x > 0`). Likewise `above_9ema` is mixed within 9-EMA bucket 2. The §A9 discipline score and §B Section-1 cards use the strict binary and must **not** be derived from bucket index.

**B. Read-fast (the trader flips through dozens of trades).**

5. Count, win rate, and net P&L are on every bucket card without expansion or hover.
6. "Near the level" (VWAP bucket 3 / 9-EMA bucket 2) is visually distinct from "extended" buckets at a glance — the visual treatment (color, opacity, position weight) per §G makes the at-vs-extended binary readable without parsing numbers.
7. `n=X` shows on every bucket card regardless of count.

**C. Sample-size honesty (a 100%-win-rate n=2 bucket is harmful).**

8. n<5 buckets render the §C "low sample" badge.
9. Expectancy is suppressed ("—") on n<5 buckets (§C / §A8).
10. Win rate still displays on n<5 buckets but is visually marked low-confidence — flagged, not hidden (§C "trusted to read the n-counts").
11. n=0 buckets still render in grid position with the §G empty treatment (grayed `0`, never blank).

**D. Interaction parity (with the §B LOCKED inline-expansion).**

12. Accordion open/close matches the §B LOCKED rule: one expansion open per section, ~200ms ease-out, pushes content down, other sections stay mounted.
13. The expanded trade table's columns, sort, and row-click → read-only trade detail sheet match Section 2's use of the shared component.
14. Expanding/collapsing a band preserves the independent filter state (§D) — it does not reset the filter bar.
